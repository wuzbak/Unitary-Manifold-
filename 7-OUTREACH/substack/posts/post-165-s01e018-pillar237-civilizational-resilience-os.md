## The core claim — and its honest boundaries

Pillar 237 does one thing: it gives civilizational continuity planning a
deterministic, auditable score sheet.

No policy agenda is embedded. No prediction of collapse is made. The module takes
explicit inputs — grid uptime fractions, hospital surge capacity, fiscal reserves —
and returns a **Resilience Readiness Index** in [0, 1] with ranked bottleneck gaps
and ROI-sorted intervention priorities.

That is what the code does. Nothing more.

---

## What the baseline says (2026 global estimate)

The module's default scenario captures a system with:

- **interagency coordination score: 0.56** — below the level required for integrated emergency response
- **fiscal reserve: 3 months** vs. a 9-month continuity target
- **hospital surge capacity: 65 000 beds** vs. 100 000 required
- **strategic food reserve: 40 days** vs. a 90-day target
- **cyber mean-time-to-detect: 72 hours** vs. a 24-hour target

The resulting Resilience Readiness Index clusters around **0.30–0.40**. This
does not mean civilization is on the verge of collapse. It means the scoring
function, applied to these inputs, returns a value indicating substantial
unaddressed gaps across multiple sectors simultaneously.

If you disagree with the input numbers, change them. The scoring function is
transparent and deterministic.

## The twelve bottleneck domains

Grid stability · Hospital surge · Supply chain · Water security · Food reserves ·
Cyber resilience · Mobility & logistics · Disaster response · Information integrity ·
Essential-service equity · Workforce readiness · Fiscal buffer.

Plus three strategic hurdles: coordination fragmentation, infrastructure fragility,
trust & governance erosion.

Every gap is computed as a simple, inspectable ratio. No black-box weights.

---

## Intervention ROI ranking

Given a budget (default: $12 B), the module ranks all 15 gaps by ROI per dollar.
The output is a sorted list — not a political recommendation. A team with different
priorities can re-sort by absolute gap size instead.

The Monte Carlo layer (200 trials, ±5–10% input perturbation) produces p10/median/p90
readiness bands, ensuring the ranking is not a fragile point estimate.

- Not a prediction that civilization will fail.

- Not a hardgate UM physics claim.

systematically anti-correlated with observed continuity outcomes under independent
validation, the module fails on its own terms and should be replaced.
