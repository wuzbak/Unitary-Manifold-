## The Problem That Started This

Why does time only move forward?

This question sounds simple. It is not. The fundamental laws of physics — Maxwell's equations, quantum mechanics, general relativity — are all time-symmetric. Run the movie backwards and the equations still hold. Yet the universe has a relentless, universal, non-negotiable directionality. Yesterday is fixed; tomorrow is not. Entropy increases; hot coffee does not spontaneously reheat itself. Causes precede effects.

The standard answer is statistical: the universe started in an improbably low-entropy state, and because there are overwhelmingly more ways to be disordered than ordered, disorder increases on average. The arrow of time is not fundamental — it is the universe exploring configuration space and finding more of it disordered.

This answer is correct. It is also incomplete. It explains the statistical tendency of the arrow. It does not explain why the arrow never fails, not even once, not even at the quantum level, not even in principle. Something more is going on.

The Unitary Manifold proposes: **the arrow of time is geometric**. The shape of the universe makes irreversibility *mandatory*, not merely *probable*.
