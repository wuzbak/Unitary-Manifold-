## Why this pillar exists

The bottleneck list is clear: safety/liability uncertainty, grid constraints, trust decline, and twelve hard technical/operational blockers. What is usually missing is **calculation discipline**.

Pillar 227 adds that discipline:

- Every hurdle is scored on a normalized `[0,1]` gap scale.
- Every bottleneck is computed from explicit measurable inputs (hours, costs, rates, power, staffing, lag years).
- Strategic and technical gaps are aggregated into one readiness index.
- Uncertainty is handled with Monte Carlo, not hand-waving.

No "it feels close" language. We compute.

---

## The 3 strategic hurdles (computed)

`src/core/pillar227_ai_robotics_bottleneck_engine.py` computes:

1. **Safety & liability framework gap**  
   Inputs: standards coverage, liability clarity, humanoid mass (150–200 lbs risk band).

2. **Infrastructure & grid readiness gap**  
   Inputs: available vs required GW, utility interconnection delay.

3. **Human trust & perception erosion**  
   Inputs: public trust %, bias incidents, uncanny discomfort %.

Each returns a normalized gap score where `0 = no blocking gap`, `1 = severe blocker`.

## The 12 bottlenecks (computed)

Pillar 227 converts each bottleneck into a formula:

1. Training data scarcity (`real_hours/target_hours` deficit)
2. Battery endurance (`runtime/required_runtime` deficit)
3. End-effector dexterity (success-rate deficit)
4. Compute-to-power conflict (compute share deficit)
5. Memory bandwidth latency (bandwidth deficit)
6. Supply-chain fragmentation (`1 - standardized_component_fraction`)
7. Weak generalization (novel-task success deficit)
8. Cybersecurity exposure (critical findings overshoot)
9. Process instability (`1 - documented_process_fraction`)
10. Global talent gap (cross-domain staffing deficit)
11. Cost of prototyping (unit-cost overshoot)
12. Software-to-hardware gap (lag-year overshoot)

## Monte Carlo stress test (5,000 runs)

We then perturb key uncertain inputs (battery runtime, software/hardware lag, trust %, training data hours, grid power availability, interconnection delay):

- **Mean readiness:** `0.3595`
- **P10 / P50 / P90:** `0.3414 / 0.3593 / 0.3774`
- **Min / Max:** `0.3283 / 0.4074`

This is the main point: uncertainty does not magically move deployment into a "ready" regime. Under reasonable 2026 perturbations, readiness stays low.
