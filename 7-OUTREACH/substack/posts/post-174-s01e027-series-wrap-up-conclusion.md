## What I Learned About Working in Public

The series taught me something I didn't expect.

Working in public — committing a derivation in one post, linking it to a test suite, having readers arrive and ask sharp questions — creates a kind of pressure that actually improves quality. Not the social pressure of performance, but the epistemological pressure of having made a claim that can be checked.

Every time I wrote "the framework predicts X" in a post, I knew that someone could clone the repository and run the test that verified it. That knowledge changes how you write. You can't hide vagueness inside prose when the prose points to executable code. The tests are the accountability structure. The public series is the human-readable layer above it.

I think this is what scientific publication should feel like: not a document that certifies completion, but a record that makes checking possible.
