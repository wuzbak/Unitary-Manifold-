Pillars 237–242 gave us sector calculators and first-pass synthesis.

Pillar 252 adds what strategy actually needs: a time-evolving digital twin that
couples climate, food, disease response, infrastructure stability, warning
coverage, and governance response into trajectory-grade outputs.

### What changes with a digital twin

A static score can tell you where you are. A dynamic twin tells you where you are
likely heading under coupling pressure and intervention choices.

Pillar 252 computes:

- a six-sector adequacy state,
- a cross-sector coupling matrix,
- a coherence index with HILS trust weighting,
- a one-year state update operator,
- multi-year simulation paths,
- budget allocation by cascade impact,
- deterministic uncertainty envelope.

### Why this matters

Global planning failures are often coupling failures: food shocks propagate into
health stress, infrastructure instability amplifies warning failure, governance
lag compounds all of it.

Pillar 252 is built to model that propagation explicitly.

### Honest boundary

This is not deterministic prophecy. It is scenario-grade planning infrastructure
with explicit uncertainty and explicit falsification criteria.

### Falsification condition

If retrospective, pre-registered benchmarking repeatedly shows that Pillar 252
trajectory ordering is anti-correlated with observed cross-sector resilience
outcomes, the pillar fails and must be revised.
