## Experiment 1: LiteBIRD — Cosmic Birefringence

**What it measures:** LiteBIRD is a JAXA satellite, with a planned launch around 2032, designed to measure the polarization of the cosmic microwave background with unprecedented precision. One of its primary science targets is cosmic birefringence — the rotation of the polarization plane of CMB photons as they travel from the surface of last scattering to us. A non-zero birefringence angle β would indicate that photons traveling through the universe rotate their polarization, which in standard physics is impossible unless there is a parity-violating axion-like field coupled to electromagnetism, or — in the Unitary Manifold's case — a geometric effect from the braided KK winding structure.

The admissible window is [0.22°, 0.38°]. There is a forbidden gap at [0.29°, 0.31°] — a prediction that the birefringence angle will specifically *not* land in that interval.

**Verdict routing:**
- **PASS:** β falls within [0.22°, 0.38°] and outside [0.29°, 0.31°]
- **TENSION:** β falls within [0.22°, 0.38°] but inside the forbidden gap [0.29°, 0.31°]
- **FALSIFIED:** β falls outside [0.22°, 0.38°]

**Why this is the primary falsifier:** The birefringence angle is uniquely sensitive to the K_CS = 74 structure. No other aspect of the UM framework produces this specific prediction in this specific way. A LiteBIRD measurement of β = 0.50° or β = 0.10° would be incompatible with the braid geometry regardless of any other adjustment. This is the prediction that cannot be escaped.
