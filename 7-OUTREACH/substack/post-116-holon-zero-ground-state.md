## What Is a Holon?

Arthur Koestler coined the word in 1967. A *holon* is a whole that is simultaneously part of a larger whole. An atom is complete in itself — it has structure, energy, stability — and it is simultaneously a component of a molecule. A molecule is whole and simultaneously part of a cell. A mind is whole and simultaneously part of a civilization.

Koestler's insight was that reality is not a hierarchy of parts but a *holarchy* of wholes. Nothing is merely a part. Nothing is merely a container. Every level is complete at its own scale and embedded in completeness at the next scale.

Holon Zero is the ground state of this holarchy. It is not the first pillar of the Unitary Manifold — it is what the first pillar rests on. It is not n_w = 5 — it is the silence from which n_w = 5 first became audible.
