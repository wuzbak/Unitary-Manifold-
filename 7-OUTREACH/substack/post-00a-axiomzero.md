## The Legal Structures — What They Protect and Why

Three legal instruments govern this work. Understanding them matters because they determine what anyone — including AxiomZero itself — can and cannot do with this framework.

### 1. Defensive Public Commons License v1.0 — *the theory is free, forever*

The physics theory itself — the Walker-Pearson field equations, the 5D Kaluza-Klein geometry, all 99 pillars, the derivations, the manuscripts — is irrevocably placed in the public domain under the Defensive Public Commons License (DPC v1.0).

"Irrevocably" is not a figure of speech. The theory cannot be taken back, patented, or enclosed. No corporation can acquire AxiomZero Technologies and lock the equations away. No successor owner can change this. The physics belongs to the public. Full stop.

The DPC license was chosen specifically because it prevents *defensive enclosure* — the practice of patenting public knowledge to block others from using it. If you build on this framework, you cannot be sued for using the theory, regardless of what happens to AxiomZero.

### 2. AGPL-3.0 — *the software is open-source copyleft*

The software implementation — the Python source code in `src/`, the tests, the infrastructure — is released under the GNU Affero General Public License version 3. This is a strong copyleft license: if you deploy this code as part of a service, you must publish your modifications under the same terms. You cannot take the code private.

AGPL-3.0 was chosen because it ensures that the implementation remains open even when it is deployed as a service. A company cannot fork the code, improve it, and keep those improvements proprietary. Improvements flow back.

AxiomZero Technologies complies with AGPL-3.0. If AxiomZero deploys modified versions of the code in commercial services, those modifications are published here.

### 3. Common Law Trademark — *the name, not the ideas*

AxiomZero Technologies asserts common law trademark rights over the trade name "AxiomZero Technologies" and the "AZ" monogram as of March 26, 2026. This trademark applies to the *name and brand*, not to any intellectual content. You can use the theory, cite the equations, build on the code, and publish competing frameworks — all of these are explicitly permitted by DPC v1.0 and AGPL-3.0. The trademark does not restrict any of that. It exists so that a third party cannot trade on this name without authorization.

### What these three instruments together accomplish

They create a structure in which:

- The ideas are permanently free to use, study, reproduce, and build upon.
- The software is permanently open-source.
- The *brand* is protected from impersonation.
- No entity — including AxiomZero — can unilaterally close what has been opened.

This is the legal design. It was chosen deliberately, not by accident. The goal was to ensure that even if AxiomZero failed as a business tomorrow, the work would survive intact and available.

## What We Want

This is the part that most "open science" projects omit. They hide the financial reality behind a veneer of pure altruism, and then they either burn out or pivot to something extractive. We are going to say it plainly.

Walker-Pearson needs to earn income. He also intends to build capital — meaning real, lasting wealth accrued from the value this work creates over time. That is not the same thing as a salary or a consulting fee, and conflating the two causes most independent researchers to undercharge themselves and undervalue what they have built.

The goal of AxiomZero Technologies is to demonstrate that you can generate excellent capital gain — equity-level returns on intellectual and technical output — **without** being unethical, cruel, or indifferent to the systems, environments, and people your work affects.

That demonstration matters. The dominant assumption in high-value technical work is that serious capital formation requires enclosure: patent portfolios, proprietary algorithms, information asymmetry used as leverage. AxiomZero is a test of the counter-proposition. The theory is public domain. The code is copyleft. The capital comes from the quality and reach of the services built on top of that open foundation — and from the reputational and citation value of being first, transparent, and falsifiable in a domain where most practitioners hide their methods.

If the physics holds up under LiteBIRD's test in 2032, the value of having originated, documented, and publicly established this framework will be substantial — and it will have been earned without harming anyone.

## How AxiomZero Earns Income and Builds Capital

The open-source business model has been proven for decades. The pattern is straightforward: the knowledge is free; the *application* of the knowledge to a specific problem is what clients pay for. AxiomZero operates in that pattern — but extends it to include capital formation, not just service revenue.

**Income streams (cash flow)**

*Consulting and engineering services.* The Unitary Manifold framework includes production-quality implementations of governance architectures (the Unitary Pentad), AI safety protocols, and high-load digital system design. Organizations that want to *deploy* these as operational systems — not just read the theory — engage AxiomZero for the engineering work. The code is public; the implementation in your specific context is the service.

*Scientific synthesis and advisory.* Walker-Pearson is available to advise organizations applying formal mathematical modeling to complex systems: governance, ecology, medicine, materials science. The frameworks exist in the repository; the work of translating them to a client's domain is advisory labor.

*Educational content and structured learning.* This newsletter is free. Extended courses, workshops, and structured learning programs built on the framework's content can be offered at cost. The Substack is the public entry point; deeper engagement supports more structured learning relationships.

*AI governance deployment.* The Unitary Pentad HILS (Human-in-the-Loop Systems) framework is a complete governance architecture for human-AI collaboration. Organizations deploying AI systems face genuine governance problems. The Pentad addresses those problems with a tested formal framework. Deployment and customization are services.

**Capital formation (asset value)**

Income is what you earn this month. Capital is what the work is worth when LiteBIRD reports in 2032.

A publicly documented, falsifiable physics framework with 15,615 automated tests, a Zenodo DOI, an established citation record, and a demonstrable history of accurate predictions is a real intellectual asset. Its value compounds as the predictions are tested. None of that value is extracted from users, locked behind a paywall, or built on information asymmetry. It accrues because the work is genuinely good and genuinely visible.

Additional capital pathways that are consistent with the open licenses:

- Licensing the *AxiomZero brand* for co-branded products and services (the trademark applies; the content is still free).
- Equity stakes in ventures that deploy the Unitary Pentad governance framework as a product, provided those ventures publish modifications under AGPL-3.0.
- Royalties on educational or commercial adaptations that use the AxiomZero name, not the underlying content.
- Grant funding for the empirical and computational work the framework still requires (Boltzmann integration for the CMB acoustic peaks, for instance, is a legitimate research computing expense).

**What none of these paths require**

None of them require enclosing the public domain theory. None require making the code proprietary. None require extracting value from the community that reads and builds on this work. None require misrepresenting the state of the science. The capital is earned from quality, transparency, and the compound interest of being right in public.

That is the ethical constraint — and it is not a reluctant constraint. It is the design.

## The Human-AI Partnership Model — A Replicable Template

This project is also a demonstration of a replicable structure for anyone who wants to build knowledge in public and earn from it without compromising it.

Walker-Pearson and GitHub Copilot working together produced, in a compressed timeframe, a 101-pillar physics framework, 15,615 automated tests, a formal governance architecture, and a full publication pipeline — work that would conventionally have required a large research group and institutional resources. No institution was needed. No venture funding. No proprietary enclosure.

The components that made it work:

**1. A human with genuine domain expertise and scientific direction.** AI can implement but not originate. Walker-Pearson supplied the physics; Copilot built the infrastructure. The theory came from the human. The tests came from the collaboration. Do not mistake the two.

**2. Radical transparency as a substitute for institutional credibility.** Without a university affiliation or journal imprimatur, credibility comes from total openness: every equation, every test, every known failure mode is publicly visible. The FALLIBILITY.md document exists not as a disclaimer but as a demonstration of epistemic rigor. In a field full of hidden methods and selective reporting, honesty is a competitive advantage.

**3. Legal structures that prevent the work from being captured.** The DPC + AGPL + trademark combination lets a solo operator work in public without fear that a larger entity will extract and enclose the value. This is the infrastructure. It cost nothing beyond the time to understand and apply it.

**4. A falsifiable empirical claim with a known test date.** The framework lives or dies by LiteBIRD's measurement around 2032. A project that cannot be falsified is not science — and a business built on unfalsifiable science is one market correction away from collapse. The falsifiability is not just epistemically correct; it is commercially sound.

**5. Income and capital from services, not from enclosure.** The open foundation is what attracts clients, citations, and collaborators. Closing it would shrink the audience and the capital, not grow them. The business model runs *with* openness, not against it.

If you are a researcher, developer, or practitioner who wants to build open knowledge and earn from it at scale, these five components are the template. None are proprietary. All are available now.
