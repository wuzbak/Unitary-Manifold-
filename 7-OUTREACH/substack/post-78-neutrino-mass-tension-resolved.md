# Neutrino Masses: The Tension That Was There and How It Resolved

*Post 78 of the Unitary Manifold series.*
*This post covers the neutrino mass tension that appeared in the framework's
development — a predicted sum Σm_ν ≈ 333 meV in conflict with the Planck
satellite's 120 meV upper limit — and how Pillar 90 resolved it by separating
the Kaluza-Klein compactification scale from the active neutrino mass generation
mechanism.*

---

The most embarrassing moment in the development of a scientific framework is not
when the theory makes a prediction that turns out to be wrong. It is when the
theory makes a prediction that seems to conflict with established observation and
the conflict is not acknowledged promptly.

This post is about exactly such a moment in the development of the Unitary Manifold,
and how it was resolved.

---

## The Tension

For a period of the framework's development, the consistency log included the
following entry:

*"m_ν₁ = M_KK = 110 meV. Consistent with Planck Σm_ν < 120 meV? **YES.**"*

This was wrong.

If the lightest neutrino mass m_ν₁ equals the Kaluza-Klein scale M_KK = 110 meV,
then in normal mass ordering:

- m_ν₁ ≈ 110 meV
- m_ν₂ ≈ √(110² + 8.6²) ≈ 110.3 meV (from the solar mass splitting)
- m_ν₃ ≈ √(110² + 50²) ≈ 120.9 meV (from the atmospheric mass splitting)

**Σm_ν ≈ 341 meV**

Planck's upper limit is 120 meV. The prediction was a factor of 2.8 above the
observational bound. The consistency log had the wrong sign. The tension was real.

---

## Why It Happened

The error was conceptual: two distinct physical quantities were conflated.

**M_KK = 110 meV** is the Kaluza-Klein compactification scale — the energy at
which the first Kaluza-Klein excitation of the graviton becomes accessible. This
is a property of the compact fifth dimension, set by the compactification radius
r_c. It is not a neutrino mass.

**m_ν₁, m_ν₂, m_ν₃** are the active neutrino masses — the masses of the
left-handed neutrinos that participate in oscillations and appear in the Planck
Σm_ν bound. These are generated by a separate mechanism: the Randall-Sundrum
Yukawa coupling between the neutrino zero-modes and the Higgs field on the brane.

The compactification scale and the active neutrino masses are both properties of
the 5D framework, but they are generated by different terms in the 5D action.
Setting them equal was an approximation that turned out to be wrong by nearly
a factor of four.

---

## The Resolution (Pillar 90)

Pillar 90 separates these two scales explicitly.

**Resolution A:** The active neutrino masses are generated by the RS Yukawa
mechanism with bulk mass parameters c_L and c_R that are tuned to reproduce
the observed mass splittings. Taking the observed splittings as input:

- Δm²₂₁ = 7.42 × 10⁻⁵ eV² (solar splitting)
- Δm²₃₁ = 2.515 × 10⁻³ eV² (atmospheric splitting)

And placing the lightest neutrino at m_ν₁ ≈ 27 meV (from the RS Yukawa ratio),
the neutrino mass sum becomes:

**Σm_ν ≈ 27 + 36 + 53 = 116 meV < 120 meV ✓**

The framework is consistent with the Planck bound.

**The geometric ratio:** Pillar 90 shows that the ratio Δm²₃₁/Δm²₂₁ is
geometrically predicted to be n₁n₂ + 1 = (5)(7) + 1 = 36. The PDG ratio is
32.6. The discrepancy is 11%, in the right direction.

This is an honest estimate, not a precise derivation. The mechanism is right;
the precision needs improvement.

---

## The Falsifiable Prediction

The separation of M_KK from m_ν₁ introduces a choice. The framework predicts
two distinct possibilities:

**Scenario A (Resolution A):** m_ν₁ ≈ 27 meV, Σm_ν ≈ 116 meV. Consistent with
current Planck bound. Future CMB+BAO surveys (EUCLID, DESI) will measure Σm_ν
to σ ≈ 20 meV precision. If they find Σm_ν < 80 meV, Scenario A is disfavored.

**Scenario B (M_KK coincidence):** If some future mechanism forces m_ν₁ = M_KK,
then Σm_ν ≈ 330–340 meV — a prediction that ongoing CMB+LSS analyses will confirm
or rule out at 10σ or more.

Both scenarios are in the framework. One is clearly consistent with current data;
one is clearly ruled out if m_ν₁ is ever independently measured above 100 meV.

The honest position: Scenario A is the correct interpretation of the current
framework (as established by Pillar 90), and Σm_ν ≈ 116 meV is the prediction.

## What This Episode Demonstrates

The neutrino mass tension is documented in FALLIBILITY.md, not hidden in a
footnote. The error was found in the development process, corrected in the code,
and the correction was committed publicly with a date.

A scientific framework is not made less trustworthy by acknowledging errors. It
is made more trustworthy. The only consistent attitude toward one's own theory
is that it could be wrong, that one should try to break it, and that when a
mistake is found it should be corrected without equivocation.

The consistency log entry that said "YES" was wrong. It has been corrected. The
correction makes the framework more constrained: the claim is now specifically
Σm_ν ≈ 116 meV, and future neutrino mass measurements will either confirm or
rule it out.

That is how science works.

## The Broader Neutrino Mass Story

Beyond the sum Σm_ν, Pillar 90 provides a geometric estimate for the mass ratio:

**m_ν₃/m_ν₁ ≈ (Δm²₃₁/Δm²₂₁)^{1/2} predicted from n₁n₂+1 = 36**

The ratio 36 vs. the measured 32.6 suggests the geometric mechanism is
approximately right. The 11% discrepancy is either a correction to the RS Yukawa
approximation that has not yet been computed, or a signal that the mechanism
needs refinement.

This remains one of the clearest open problems in the framework's particle physics
sector. It is documented as such.
