## The 28 Parameters

The Standard Model's free parameters fall into five groups:

**Gauge couplings (3):** α_em (fine structure), α_s (strong), sin²θ_W
(electroweak mixing). These set the overall strength of each force.

**Higgs sector (2):** The Higgs mass m_H and the vacuum expectation value v.
These set the mass scale for everything that couples to the Higgs.

**Quark masses and mixing (10):** Six quark masses (u, d, s, c, b, t), three
CKM mixing angles (θ₁₂, θ₁₃, θ₂₃), and one CP-violating phase δ_CKM.

**Lepton masses and mixing (7):** Three charged lepton masses (e, μ, τ), three
PMNS mixing angles (θ₁₂, θ₁₃, θ₂₃^PMNS), and one CP-violating phase δ_CP^PMNS.

**Neutrino sector (3):** Two mass-squared splittings Δm²₂₁ and Δm²₃₁, and the
overall lightest neutrino mass m_ν₁ (assuming normal ordering).

**Cosmological constant (1):** Technically a separate parameter from the SM, but
no consistent theory can ignore it.

Counting gives 26–28 depending on whether Majorana phases are included.
