## The exact falsification conditions

These have been stated in every post in this series. They are stated again here,
for the last time, as a commitment:

**Complete falsification:** β outside [0.22°, 0.38°] at LiteBIRD's ~0.05° precision.
The framework is ruled out in its current form. The specific Chern-Simons mechanism
for the arrow of time is wrong.

**Mechanism falsification:** β within [0.29°, 0.31°] — the gap between the two
predicted channels. The overall birefringence prediction is right (β ≈ 0.3°) but the
braided-winding mechanism (which predicts values *outside* the gap) is wrong.

**CS coupling falsification:** β consistent with zero at σ ≤ 0.05°. The CS coupling
mechanism is ruled out entirely. The spectral index agreement may be coincidental
with a different mechanism.

**Strong confirmation:** β ≈ 0.35° with σ(β) ≤ 0.05°, ruling out k_CS = 73 and 75.
This would be the strongest evidence the framework has yet received for the CS level
selection.

## What happens if it's wrong

Let's be specific.

If LiteBIRD measures β outside the window:

**The birefringence prediction is wrong.** This is the primary falsifier and we
commit to acknowledging it publicly, without equivocation. We will issue a public
statement within 90 days of the data release.

**What does not follow:** that the entire framework is nonsense. The CMB spectral
index prediction (n_s = 0.9635 within Planck 1σ) came from the KK Jacobian of the
dimensional reduction. That result does not depend on the specific birefringence
mechanism. If β is wrong, n_s may still be right by a different mechanism. The
two predictions are not logically coupled.

**What else does not follow:** that the problem of time is unsolved. The problem —
why does the universe have an arrow of time? — is real and important regardless of
whether this specific geometric mechanism is correct. A falsified framework still
contributes to the answer by eliminating one possibility precisely.

**What also does not follow:** that the mathematical structure is without value.
The five-dimensional Kaluza-Klein geometry with an irreversibility field is a
well-defined mathematical structure. If it doesn't describe our universe, it may
describe another one (in the winding-pair catalog), or it may be the starting
point for a revised structure that does work. Falsified theories are often the
most useful stepping stones to correct ones.

## What remains true if LiteBIRD says no

**The FTUM fixed-point theorem** is a mathematical result. It says that contraction
operators have unique fixed points. This remains true.

**The information conservation law** (∇_μJ^μ_inf = 0 follows from Noether applied
to U(1) symmetry of φ) remains structurally valid within the 5D framework. Whether
the 5D framework describes our universe is the open question.

**The dimensional reduction structure** (G_AB → g_μν) is the standard Kaluza-Klein
mechanism, known mathematics since Kaluza and Klein (1919-1921). This doesn't go away.

**The human-AI collaboration** that produced this repository stands as a documented
example of what that collaboration can do: generate specific, quantitative, falsifiable
predictions from a theoretical framework, implement them in code, test them for
internal consistency, and commit to the results before knowing the answer.

Whether the answer is yes or no, that process is demonstrated. It works.

## The thing that doesn't go away

The question that started this framework — why does time have a direction? — is
real regardless of whether this specific answer is correct.

It is one of the deepest questions in physics. It has not been answered by the
standard model. It has not been answered by general relativity. It has not been
answered by quantum mechanics. It will not be answered by LiteBIRD saying no to
this specific framework — it will remain open, waiting for the next attempt.

The Unitary Manifold was one attempt. A serious attempt, internally consistent,
specifically predictive, honestly documented. If LiteBIRD rules it out, the next
attempt will have the benefit of knowing this one was tried and where it failed.
That is how science advances.

## A note on what this series was

This series — seventy-three posts, covering the core physics, the philosophical
implications, the religious correspondences, the institutional questions, and finally
the honest accounting of what we know and don't — was an attempt at something that
doesn't usually happen in science communication.

Not popularization, which simplifies to the point of misrepresentation.
Not advocacy, which omits the failures to make the case for the successes.
But honest communication: here is the claim, here is the derivation, here is the
gap, here is the test, here is what we do when the test says no.

If the framework survives LiteBIRD, this series will be the record of a theory
that was developed honestly and confirmed honestly. If it doesn't, this series will
be the record of a serious attempt that was falsified honestly.

Either way, it will have been worth doing.
