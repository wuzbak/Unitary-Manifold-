# 🌱 Growing Guide — Practical Cultivation for All Skill Levels

> *"The seed does not ask permission to grow. Give it warmth, water, and good soil,
> and it will show you what it knows."*

## Beginner's Principles

Before any technique, these truths underpin all successful growing:

1. **Healthy soil grows healthy plants.** Invest in soil before seeds.
2. **Observe before you intervene.** Most "problems" resolve themselves in a healthy system.
3. **Grow what works in your climate.** Don't fight the weather — choose plants that thrive in it.
4. **Start small, grow confidently.** A 4×4m bed well-managed beats 400m² of chaos.
5. **Every failure is data.** Keep notes. The best gardeners have killed the most plants.

## 1 · Planning Your Garden

### Site Assessment

Before planting anything, assess:

**Sun:** Count hours of direct sunlight in the planting area.
- Full sun: 6+ hours/day — required for most fruiting vegetables, most herbs, grains
- Partial shade: 3-6 hours — leafy greens, some roots (carrots, beets), woodland herbs (gotu kola, lemon balm)
- Full shade: <3 hours — very few food plants; focus on medicinal woodland species (ginseng, goldenseal, bloodroot)

**Water:** Where does water pool? Where does it drain? Plan accordingly:
- Wet areas: grow water-loving plants (gotu kola, watercress, angelica, meadowsweet)
- Dry raised areas: Mediterranean herbs, succulents, drought-tolerant perennials

**Soil:** Do the ribbon test (see [SOIL_AND_EARTH.md](SOIL_AND_EARTH.md)). Test pH with a simple test kit.

**Aspect (hemisphere-dependent):**
- Northern hemisphere: south-facing slopes receive maximum sun
- Southern hemisphere: north-facing slopes receive maximum sun

### Zone and Sector Planning (Permaculture)

**Zones by use frequency:**
- **Zone 1** (steps from kitchen door): Daily-use herbs and salad greens. Chives, parsley, mint, basil, lettuce.
- **Zone 2** (visited weekly): Perennial vegetables, medicinal herbs, soft fruit. Comfrey, valerian, echinacea, blackcurrant.
- **Zone 3** (visited monthly): Main crops, grain, large medicinal trees. Elderberry, hawthorn, ginkgo.
- **Zone 4** (semi-wild): Foraging area, wildcrafted herbs, support species. Dandelion, nettle, plantain.
- **Zone 5** (wild): Leave untouched. Biodiversity reservoir; attracts predatory insects.

## 2 · Seed Starting and Propagation

### Direct Sowing vs. Starting Indoors

**Direct sow** (seeds sown where they will grow):
- Best for: tap-rooted plants that dislike transplanting (carrot, parsnip, dill, cilantro, poppies)
- Also good for: fast-growing annuals (beans, peas, squash, sunflower, amaranth)
- Requirement: soil warm enough for germination (see temperatures below)

**Starting indoors** (6-8 weeks before last frost date):
- Best for: slow-growing plants, heat-lovers starting in cold climates (tomatoes, peppers, basil, eggplant)
- Also essential for: expensive or rare seeds (you want every one to count)

### Germination Temperature Guide

| Category | Minimum soil temp | Optimal temp |
|---------|------------------|-------------|
| Cool-season crops (lettuces, spinach, brassicas) | 5°C (40°F) | 15-18°C (60-65°F) |
| Warm-season crops (tomatoes, peppers, cucumber) | 15°C (60°F) | 21-27°C (70-80°F) |
| Hot-season crops (basil, moringa, amaranth) | 18°C (65°F) | 24-30°C (75-85°F) |
| Root vegetables | 7°C (45°F) | 18-24°C (65-75°F) |
| Most herbs | 15°C (60°F) | 18-24°C (65-75°F) |

### Seed Viability

Most seeds remain viable for 2-5 years if stored in cool, dark, dry conditions (sealed jar in refrigerator).

**Test germination rate:** Place 10 seeds between moist paper towels at room temperature for the germination period. Count how many sprout. <70% = fresh seed needed.

### Propagation Methods

**Vegetative propagation** creates genetic copies (clones) of parent plants — essential for
perennial herbs where you want to preserve a proven plant's characteristics.

| Method | Plants | How |
|--------|--------|-----|
| **Stem cuttings** | Rosemary, lavender, sage, mint, thyme, lemon balm, comfrey | Cut 10-15cm tip; remove lower leaves; insert in moist perlite or compost; keep humid |
| **Division** | All clumping perennials (lemon balm, valerian, echinacea, astragalus) | Dig up in spring/autumn; split crown with knife or spade; replant immediately |
| **Root cuttings** | Comfrey, horseradish, Japanese knotweed | 3-5cm root sections buried horizontally in spring |
| **Runners** | Gotu kola, strawberry, mint, purslane | Pin runner to soil; roots form at node; separate when rooted |
| **Layering** | Rosemary, elderberry, hawthorn, almost any shrub | Bend a low branch to ground; wound slightly; pin with wire and cover with soil; roots in 2-6 months; sever and transplant |

## 3 · The Planting Calendar

Growing regions vary enormously — but the principles of timing are universal:

### Climate-Zone Planting Framework

**Tropical (no frost):** Two main seasons — wet (growing season) and dry (stress season).
- Wet season: plant all annuals; harvest herbs before peak rains waterlog them
- Dry season: rely on drought-tolerant perennials; moringa, aloe, agave, pigeon pea

**Subtropical (light frost):**
- Spring: plant warm-season crops after last frost; sow annuals
- Summer: maintain and harvest; plant heat-tolerant varieties
- Autumn: plant cool-season crops (best growing season)
- Winter: cool-season crops continue; perennials dormant or slow

**Temperate:**
- Late winter indoors: start tomatoes, peppers, herbs 8 weeks before last frost
- Early spring: direct sow cool-season crops (peas, lettuce, spinach, brassicas)
- After last frost: plant out warm-season crops; direct sow beans, squash, amaranth
- Summer: succession sow, harvest, maintain
- Late summer: plant brassicas for autumn/winter harvest
- Autumn: plant garlic; collect seeds; prep beds

**Mediterranean (hot dry summer, mild wet winter):**
- Autumn-Winter: the main growing season; almost everything grows well
- Summer: heat-tolerant crops (tomatoes, peppers, squash) with water; rest of garden rests

## 4 · Transplanting and Establishment

### Hardening Off

Before transplanting indoor-grown seedlings outdoors, they must be acclimatised:
- Day 1-3: 1-2 hours outside in sheltered shade
- Day 4-6: 3-4 hours including morning sun
- Day 7-10: Full outdoor day; bring in at night if frost risk
- Day 11+: Leave outside; transplant

### Transplanting

- Choose overcast day or late afternoon (minimises transplant stress)
- Water seedlings 1 hour before transplanting
- Dig hole larger than root ball
- Place at same depth as in pot (tomatoes are exception: plant deep)
- Water in well with diluted seaweed solution (promotes root establishment)
- Apply mulch around but not touching stem
- Provide wind shelter for first week if needed

### The First 4 Weeks

- Water consistently (not excessively) — check soil moisture at 5cm depth
- Hold off on feeding until roots are established (2-3 weeks)
- Watch for pest pressure — young transplants are most vulnerable
- **Do not remove leaves that are yellowing** from transplant shock; wait for new healthy growth

## 5 · Watering

**The most common mistake in gardens is overwatering.** More plants die from wet feet than drought.

### The Finger Test
Push finger 5cm into soil. If dry: water. If moist: don't. If wet: absolutely don't.

### Watering Principles

1. Water deeply and infrequently (builds deep root systems)
2. Water at the base of plants, not on leaves (prevents fungal disease)
3. Morning watering preferred (leaves dry before night; less disease)
4. Use mulch to reduce watering frequency
5. Group plants by water need (hydrozoning)

### Irrigation Methods

| Method | Water efficiency | Best for |
|--------|----------------|---------|
| Hand watering | 60-70% | Small gardens; precise control |
| Drip irrigation | 90%+ | Rows; efficient; automate with timer |
| Ollas (clay pots) | 90-95% | Traditional; zero energy; deep root watering |
| Soaker hose | 80-85% | Beds; perennial borders |
| Overhead sprinkler | 50-60% | Lawn; seedling establishment only |

## 6 · Feeding and Fertilising

Healthy living soil needs little supplementation. When plants are deficient:

### Organic Liquid Feeds

**Comfrey tea:** Steep comfrey leaves in water for 3-4 weeks. Dilute 10:1.
High potassium — excellent for fruiting plants during flowering and fruiting.

**Nettle tea:** Steep nettles for 2-3 weeks. Dilute 10:1.
High nitrogen — vegetative growth, transplant establishment.

**Worm casting tea:** Steep worm castings in water with air stone for 24 hours (aerated compost tea).
Microbial inoculant + gentle nutrients.

**Seaweed extract:** Commercial or DIY (steep dried seaweed). Micronutrients, hormones (cytokinins),
improves germination, frost resistance, stress tolerance.

### Solid Amendments

| Amendment | NPK approx | Best use |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| Compost | 1-2-1 | Universal soil improver; apply 2-5cm annually |
| Worm castings | 1-0.5-0.5 + hormones | Transplant boost; compost tea |
| Blood meal | 12-0-0 | High N; use sparingly; alkalising |
| Bone meal | 3-15-0 | Phosphorus for rooting; alkalising |
| Wood ash | 0-1-5 | Potassium + lime; alkalises soil |
| Kelp meal | 0.5-0.5-2.5 | Trace minerals; biostimulants |
| Rock dust (basalt) | 0-0-0 + trace | Slow-release all trace minerals; improves soil structure |

## 7 · Pest and Disease Management

### The First Principle: Build Ecosystem Defences

A garden with high biodiversity, good soil, and no pesticides develops its own pest control:
- Predatory insects (lacewings, hoverflies, parasitic wasps, ground beetles) eat aphids, caterpillars
- Insectivorous birds and toads eat slugs and beetles
- Beneficial fungi suppress soil pathogens

**Disrupting this ecosystem with pesticides** — even "organic" pesticides — creates boom-bust
cycles that amplify pest pressure over time.

### Plant-Based Pest Controls

| Pest | Plant-based control | How to use |
|------|---------------------|------------|
| Aphids | Neem oil spray (0.5%), garlic/chilli spray, pyrethrum | Spray undersides of leaves at dusk |
| Spider mites | Neem oil, rosemary oil spray | Weekly spray; remove worst-affected leaves |
| Fungal disease (mildew) | Baking soda + neem oil spray, comfrey tea spray | Weekly preventive spray |
| Slugs and snails | Crushed eggshell barrier, copper tape, iron phosphate bait, ground ginger deterrent | Evening application; coffee grounds may help |
| Cabbage white caterpillars | Bacillus thuringiensis (BT) spray | Biological; specific to caterpillars |
| Root pests | Diatomaceous earth in soil; marigold companion planting (*Tagetes* — true French/African marigold) | Plant marigolds densely around susceptible crops |

### Companion Planting

The practice of growing plants together for mutual benefit — based on chemical signalling, pest
confusion, pollinator attraction, or nutrient cycling.

| Companion pair | Benefit |
|----------------|---------|
| Basil + tomato | Basil volatile oils repel aphids and tomato hornworm; improves fruit flavour |
| Nasturtium + brassicas | Nasturtium acts as aphid "trap crop"; sacrifices itself |
| Dill + brassicas | Attracts predatory wasps that parasitise cabbage worms |
| Borage + strawberry / brassica | Deters tomato hornworm; attracts pollinators |
| Chamomile + any crop | Accumulates calcium and potassium; improves neighbour flavour and health |
| Comfrey + fruit trees | Dynamic accumulator; mulch leaves; provides K, Ca, Mg |
| Garlic + roses | Deters aphids; antifungal volatile oils |
| Three Sisters (corn + bean + squash) | Bean: N fixation; squash: mulch/shade; corn: trellis for beans |
| Marigold (*Tagetes*) + tomato / pepper | Nematode deterrent in soil; repels aphids and whitefly |

## 8 · Harvesting Principles

### When to Harvest

**Herbs and leaves:** Harvest just before or at flowering — this is when essential oil and
secondary metabolite content is highest. Morning after dew has evaporated; before noon heat.

**Flowers:** At peak opening; before pollination is complete (petals still firm).

**Roots and bark:** Autumn and winter, when energy has retreated from leaves and concentrated in roots. Biennials: harvest roots in first year autumn (before flowering).

**Seeds:** When dry on the plant; rattle in seedhead; before dispersal.

**Fruit:** When fully ripe (peak nutrient and flavour content).

### The Rule of Thirds

When harvesting from perennials: **never remove more than 1/3 of the plant** at any one time.
Exceptions: annual crops (harvest entire plant at end of season).

### Drying and Preservation

**Air drying:**
- Bundle stems; hang upside down in dark, dry, well-ventilated space
- Temperature 25-35°C ideal
- Avoid direct sun (degrades essential oils and chlorophyll)
- Test: leaf crumbles when rubbed between fingers (not just limp)

**Dehydrator:** Set at 35-40°C for volatile herbs (lavender, chamomile, mint); up to 50°C for roots. Better colour and oil retention than air drying.

**Freeze drying:** Gold standard for medicinal potency but expensive equipment.

**Tincturing fresh:** Some plants retain highest potency when tinctured fresh (immediately after harvest) vs. dried. Key plants: lemon balm, St John's Wort, echinacea, California poppy.

## 9 · Perennial Food Forest Design

A food forest mimics the structure of a woodland ecosystem to create a low-maintenance,
self-sustaining garden that produces food, medicine, and materials indefinitely.

### The Seven Layers

| Layer | Height | Example plants |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| **Canopy** | 7m+ | Apple, pear, hawthorn, mulberry, walnut, ginkgo (wild food/medicine trees) |
| **Sub-canopy** | 2-7m | Elderberry, hazel, sea buckthorn, moringa (warm), coppiced alder |
| **Shrub** | 0.5-2m | Gooseberry, currant, rosemary, lavender, ashwagandha, echinacea |
| **Herbaceous** | 0-0.5m | Comfrey, valerian, yarrow, calendula, nettle, herbs |
| **Ground cover** | <0.1m | Thyme, creeping chamomile, clover, purslane, gotu kola |
| **Rhizosphere** | Underground | Garlic, burdock, Jerusalem artichoke, dandelion, horseradish |
| **Climber** | Uses structure | Hops, passionflower, Schisandra, kiwi, grape |

### Guild Design Example

A hawthorn-centred medicinal guild:
- **Centre:** Hawthorn (*Crataegus monogyna*) — cardiovascular medicine; food
- **Near:** Comfrey — dynamic accumulator; mulch supply
- **Near:** Yarrow — mineral accumulator; pest repellent; wound healing
- **Near:** Valerian — attracts earthworms; attracts beneficial insects; sleep medicine
- **Ground cover:** Thyme — aromatic pest repellent; antimicrobial
- **Climber:** Hops (north-facing side) — sleep, anxiety; also female flower for brewing

## 10 · Greenhouse and Indoor Growing

Not everyone has outdoor space. The following grow well indoors or under lights:

**Windowsill (4+ hours direct sun):**
- Herbs: basil, thyme, rosemary (south-facing windows), chives, parsley, mint
- Microgreens: any variety; 15cm trays; 7-14 days
- Sprouts: broccoli, lentil, mung bean; 3-5 days in jar

**Under grow lights (16 hours light / 8 hours dark):**
- Full-spectrum LED, 200-300W for 1m²
- Lettuce, spinach, herbs, microgreens, gotu kola, bacopa (aquatic)
- Moringa (container; prune to maintain height)

**Aquatic / semi-aquatic:**
- Bacopa monnieri — in shallow tray of water
- Watercress — in shallow flowing or changed water
- Gotu kola — moist potting mix; tropical temperatures
