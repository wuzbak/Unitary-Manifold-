## What Makes a Superfood?

For this guide, a "superfood" must satisfy all three criteria:

1. **Nutritional density** — exceptionally high in micronutrients, protein, or bioactive compounds relative to calories
2. **Grow-ability** — can be grown by a non-expert, in a wide range of climates, with minimal inputs
3. **Resilience** — tolerates drought, poor soil, heat, or cold better than mainstream crops

No single food is a complete diet. The following plants complement each other; grow several.

## Tier 1 — Grow Anywhere, Eat Everyday

### 1 · Moringa (*Moringa oleifera*) — "The Miracle Tree"

**Origin:** India, sub-Saharan Africa  
**Climate:** Tropical to subtropical; tolerates mild frost if roots are established (USDA zones 9-11 outdoors, container anywhere)

**Nutritional profile (gram for gram, fresh leaves):**
- Vitamin C: 7× orange
- Vitamin A: 10× carrot
- Calcium: 17× milk
- Potassium: 15× banana
- Iron: 25× spinach
- Protein: complete (all 9 essential amino acids); leaves are ~30% protein by dry weight

**Key bioactives:** Isothiocyanates (MIC-4, MIC-9 — anti-inflammatory), quercetin, kaempferol, chlorogenic acid, glucosinolates, zeatin (cytokinin — anti-ageing)

**Growing:**
- Grow from seed or cutting; extremely fast-growing (3-5m in first year)
- In cold climates: grow in large pots; cut to 1m before first frost; overwinter indoors; cut-and-come-again
- Drought-tolerant once established; poor-soil tolerant
- Edible: young leaves (raw or cooked), pods ("drumsticks"), flowers, seeds, roots (horseradish flavour)

**Harvest:** Cut new growth regularly to keep bushy and productive. Dry leaves at low temperature (<50°C) for powder.

### 2 · Amaranth (*Amaranthus* spp.) — Ancient Grain + Greens

**Origin:** Mesoamerica  
**Climate:** USDA zones 2-11; exceptional heat and drought tolerance

**Nutritional profile:**
- Complete protein grain: lysine-rich (rare in plant grains); 13-18% protein
- High in: iron, magnesium, calcium, zinc, manganese, phosphorus
- High in: squalene (antioxidant, skin health)
- Leaves: higher in calcium and iron than spinach; eaten like spinach

**Key bioactives:** Squalene, peptides (lunasin-like), rutin (quercetin-3-rutinoside), betacyanins (red varieties)

**Growing:**
- Direct sow after last frost; thin to 30cm apart
- Grows in poor, dry soil where most crops fail
- Heat-loving; bolts in cool weather (then harvest seeds)
- Both grain (panicle heads) and greens harvested
- One plant produces 50,000-500,000 seeds

### 3 · Sweet Potato (*Ipomoea batatas*) — The Easiest Calorie-Dense Root

**Origin:** South America  
**Climate:** USDA zones 9-12 as perennial; anywhere as annual

**Nutritional profile:**
- Rich in beta-carotene (orange varieties — 1 medium = 400% RDA Vitamin A)
- Purple varieties: exceptionally high anthocyanins (delphinidin, peonidin) — anti-inflammatory, neuroprotective
- B vitamins, vitamin C, potassium, manganese
- Lower glycaemic index than regular potato (especially when cooled after cooking — resistant starch)

**Leaves:** Often overlooked — the leaves are highly nutritious (protein, calcium, iron, vitamins A and C) and can be eaten as greens

**Growing:**
- Start from slips (sprouts from the root) — buy once, propagate indefinitely
- Full sun, well-drained soil; drought-tolerant once established
- No serious pests in most climates
- Harvest: 90-120 days; cure at 30°C/90% humidity for 10 days to convert starch to sugar

### 4 · Kale and Leafy Brassicas (*Brassica oleracea*) — Perennial Nutrition

**Origin:** Mediterranean coast  
**Climate:** USDA zones 7-11 (some varieties perennial); all climates as annual

**Nutritional profile:**
- Exceptionally high in: Vitamins K1 and K2, C, A, B6, folate
- Rich in: calcium, manganese, copper, potassium
- High glucosinolates → sulforaphane (see [PLANT_CHEMISTRY.md](PLANT_CHEMISTRY.md))
- Lutein and zeaxanthin: eye health (macular degeneration prevention)

**Perennial varieties:**
- **Tree kale / Walking stick kale** (*B. oleracea* Acephala group) — USDA zones 7-11; grows for years
- **Daubenton's kale** — propagates by cuttings; very productive; zones 7-10
- **Nine Star perennial broccoli** — produces heads for years in mild climates

**Growing:**
- Sow spring or autumn (bolts in summer heat; plant where it gets afternoon shade in hot climates)
- Most productive and flavourful after light frost
- Succession plant every 3-4 weeks for continuous harvest

### 5 · Legumes — The Protein and Nitrogen Foundation

Legumes are foundational in every sustainable food system because:
1. They fix atmospheric nitrogen via root-nodule *Rhizobium* bacteria (free fertiliser for surrounding plants)
2. They provide complete-ish protein when combined with grains
3. Most are drought-tolerant and grow in poor soil

| Legume | Climate | Growing ease | Key nutrients |
|--------|---------|-------------|---------------|
| **Cowpea / Black-eyed pea** | Tropical, subtropical | ★★★★★ | Protein, iron, folate; heat and drought champion |
| **Lentil** | Temperate, semi-arid | ★★★★ | Folate, iron, protein; cool-season |
| **Chickpea** | Mediterranean, semi-arid | ★★★★ | Protein, zinc, folate, fibre |
| **Soybean** | Temperate | ★★★★ | Complete protein, isoflavones, B vitamins |
| **Common bean** (*Phaseolus vulgaris*) | Universal | ★★★★★ | Protein, folate, iron, polyphenols |
| **Fava bean** | Cool temperate | ★★★★★ | Protein, L-DOPA (Parkinson's support), zinc |
| **Peanut** | Tropical, subtropical | ★★★★ | Protein, resveratrol, niacin, magnesium |
| **Pigeon pea** (*Cajanus cajan*) | Tropical | ★★★★★ | Drought-tolerant perennial; nitrogen-fixing tree |

| **Thyme** | Cooking | Thymol, carvacrol | Antimicrobial, respiratory, antioxidant |

| **Oregano** | Pizza, Mediterranean cooking | Carvacrol, thymol, rosmarinic acid | Most antioxidant-dense common herb |

## Tier 2 — High-Impact with Slightly More Care

### 7 · Spirulina / Aquatic Microgreens

**Spirulina** (*Arthrospira platensis*) — technically a cyanobacterium but cultivated like a plant:
- Dry weight: 60-70% complete protein
- Phycocyanin (blue pigment): potent anti-inflammatory, anti-tumour
- Contains: B12 (though bioavailability debated), iron, GLA, beta-carotene
- Can be grown at home in a simple container with nutrient solution and light

**Watercress** — semi-aquatic; exceptional PEITC content (anti-cancer isothiocyanate); easiest aquatic growing

### 8 · Microgreens — Maximum Nutrition in Minimum Space

Microgreens (seedlings 7-14 days old) have 4-40× the nutrient concentration of mature plants.

| Microgreen | Key standout nutrient | Growing time |
|-----------|----------------------|-------------|
| **Broccoli sprouts** | Sulforaphane (highest of any form) | 5-7 days |
| **Sunflower** | Complete protein, zinc, vitamin E | 10-12 days |
| **Peas** | Protein, vitamin C, folate | 10-14 days |
| **Radish** | Vitamin C, isothiocyanates | 7-10 days |
| **Amaranth** | Protein, iron, anthocyanins | 10-12 days |
| **Red cabbage** | Anthocyanins, vitamin C | 7-10 days |

**Growing:** Tray + potting mix + seeds + indirect light. Water from bottom to prevent damping off. No outdoor space required.

### 9 · Stinging Nettle (*Urtica dioica*)

Often considered a weed — actually one of the most nutritious plants in temperate climates:
- Iron: 5× spinach
- Calcium: 2× milk (by dry weight)
- Protein: 25-30% by dry weight (complete amino acid profile)
- Silica: highest of any common food plant
- Quercetin, kaempferol, chlorogenic acid, beta-sitosterol

**Growing:** Will grow literally anywhere that has moisture. Harvest with gloves (cooking or drying neutralises sting). Perennial — a patch gives decades of free food.

**Uses:** Nettle soup, nettle tea, blanched like spinach, dried as powder (tasteless — add to anything)

### 10 · Quinoa (*Chenopodium quinoa*)

- Complete protein (all essential amino acids including lysine)
- High saponin content (rinse before eating)
- Grows in: poor soil, high altitude, light frost, moderate drought
- Rich in magnesium, phosphorus, folate, iron
- **Leaves** are also edible (like spinach)

**Climate note:** Prefers cool to temperate climates. Struggles in high heat/humidity. Consider amaranth as the tropical equivalent.

## Tier 3 — Wildcrafted and Foraged Superfoods

### 11 · Dandelion (*Taraxacum officinale*) — The Weed That Feeds

- Leaves: vitamin A, C, K, B2, calcium, iron, beta-carotene, taraxacin (bitter), prebiotic inulin
- Root: inulin (highest concentration), bitter compounds supporting liver and digestion
- Flowers: lutein, zeaxanthin, polyphenols

**Entirely free, grows everywhere, almost impossible to eradicate** — the perfect superfood.
Young spring leaves: salad. Older leaves: sauté or dry as powder. Roots: decoction for liver.

### 12 · Purslane (*Portulaca oleracea*)

Often considered a garden weed — the highest plant source of **omega-3 ALA** (alpha-linolenic acid).
Also rich in melatonin, glutathione, vitamins E and C. Flavour: slightly lemony, succulent texture.
Grow: direct sow anywhere; essentially a self-seeding annual.
