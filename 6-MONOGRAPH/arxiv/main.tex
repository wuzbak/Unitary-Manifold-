\begin{abstract}
We present the \emph{Unitary Manifold} (UM), a five-dimensional Kaluza--Klein
framework in which thermodynamic irreversibility, information flow, and quantum
transition asymmetry are unified as projections of a single higher-dimensional
geometry.  The fifth dimension is compact, the cylinder condition
$\partial_5 G_{AB}=0$ is imposed, and the 5D metric is cast in the standard
Kaluza--Klein block form with a vector field $B_\mu$ — the \emph{irreversibility
1-form} — and a scalar $\phi$ — the \emph{entropic dilaton}.  Dimensional
reduction of the 5D Einstein--Hilbert action yields a 4D effective action
whose field equations we call the \emph{Walker--Pearson equations}.  We show
that (i) the Second Law of Thermodynamics is a geometric identity rather than a
statistical postulate; (ii) information pressure provides a purely geometric
alternative to a cosmological constant; (iii) entropic holography identifies
boundary entropy with bulk area; and (iv) a Thermodynamic Cosmic Censorship
Conjecture asserts that irreversible singularities are hidden behind holographic
boundaries.  We further develop an information-geometric quantisation scheme, a
Thermodynamic Multiverse topology, and a Final Theorem unifying irreversibility,
holography, and topology as the three pillars of observable reality.
\end{abstract}
